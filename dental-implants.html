What Are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is the closest a patient can get to a natural, healthy tooth. It consists of three primary components: the Titanium Post (implanted into the jawbone), the Abutment (the connector), and the Prosthetic Restoration (the visible crown).

Because the titanium integrates directly with your bone through a biological process called osseointegration, the implant becomes a permanent part of your anatomy. This helps prevent the bone resorption that naturally occurs after tooth loss, supporting your facial structure over time.

Why Choose Implants Over Bridges or Dentures?

While traditional bridges and dentures replace the visible part of the tooth, only implants replace the root. This offers several clinical advantages for our Jalpaiguri patients:

The Implant Procedure — Step by Step

At our Pahari Para clinic, Dr. Sudip Roy follows a structured protocol to ensure safety and a strong outcome:

  1. Initial Consultation & 3D Imaging: A detailed oral exam and 3D CBCT scan to assess bone density and plan the exact placement.
  2. Surgical Placement: Under local anesthesia, the titanium post is precisely positioned in the jawbone.
  3. Osseointegration (Healing): Over a period of months, your jawbone grows around the implant, creating a stable foundation.
  4. Abutment Placement: A small connector is attached to the post to hold your new tooth in place.
  5. Final Restoration: A custom-made crown is fixed, matching the color and shape of your natural teeth.

How long do dental implants last?

With good oral hygiene and regular check-ups, implants are designed as a long-term solution. Longevity varies by individual factors including bone health and lifestyle.

What is the difference between an implant and a bridge?

An implant is an independent titanium root that doesn't affect neighbouring teeth and helps preserve jawbone. A bridge anchors to adjacent teeth and doesn't preserve bone the same way. Dr. Roy will advise the right option for your case.
