Bone Biology 101: Osseointegration

Osseointegration refers to the direct structural and functional connection between living bone and the surface of a load-carrying implant. It is not a mechanical lock; it is a biological process that cannot be rushed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is immediate dental implant loading safe?

Immediate loading can be successful in specific cases with high bone density, but it carries higher risks. For long-term success, a delayed loading protocol allowing full osseointegration is often safer.

Why do dental implants fail after 5 years?

Late failure is often caused by 'Peri-implantitis' (inflammation around the implant), often triggered by poor oral hygiene, smoking, or aggressive implant surface textures that trap bacteria.
