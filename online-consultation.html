Smile Design & Cosmetic Dentistry

  1. What is Digital Smile Design?

    A digital planning process where your smile makeover is visualized and planned before treatment begins, helping set clear expectations.

  2. How long does a smile makeover take?

    Duration depends on the specific treatments involved; Dr. Roy will explain an estimated timeline after examining your case.

  3. Is a smile makeover painful?

    Modern techniques and anesthesia are used to minimize discomfort during cosmetic procedures.

  4. What is smile correction without braces?

    In select cases, minor alignment or spacing issues can be addressed with veneers, bonding, or clear aligners instead of traditional braces — suitability depends on individual assessment.

  5. Can teeth gaps be fixed without braces?

    Small gaps may sometimes be closed using bonding or veneers; a clinical evaluation is needed to confirm if you're a candidate.

  6. Are veneers permanent?

    Veneers are long-lasting but not lifelong; they may need replacement after some years depending on care and wear.

  7. Does teeth whitening damage enamel?

    When performed under professional supervision with appropriate materials, whitening is generally considered safe for enamel.

  8. How long do teeth whitening results last?

    Results vary by individual habits (diet, smoking, oral hygiene) and typically last several months to a couple of years.

  9. What is the difference between veneers and crowns?

    Veneers cover the front surface of a tooth for cosmetic improvement, while crowns cover the entire tooth and are often used after significant damage or root canal treatment.

  10. Can cosmetic dentistry fix a crooked smile?

    Depending on severity, options range from veneers and bonding to orthodontic treatment; an in-person exam determines the right approach.

Dental Implants

  1. What are dental implants?

    Titanium posts surgically placed in the jawbone to replace missing tooth roots, later topped with a crown.

  2. Am I a candidate for dental implants?

    Candidacy depends on bone density, gum health, and overall health — a clinical and radiographic evaluation is required.

  3. How long do dental implants last?

    With proper care and regular checkups, implants can last many years to a lifetime for many patients.

  4. Is dental implant surgery painful?

    The procedure is performed under local anesthesia; some post-operative discomfort is normal and manageable with prescribed care.

  5. What is All-on-4 / All-on-6 implants?

    Full-arch replacement techniques using 4 or 6 implants to support a complete set of fixed teeth.

  6. How long is recovery after implant placement?

    Initial healing typically takes a few days to weeks, while full bone integration can take a few months before the final crown is placed.

  7. Are dental implants safe for diabetic patients?

    Many diabetic patients with well-controlled blood sugar can be candidates; this needs individual medical evaluation.

  8. What is the difference between an implant and a bridge?

    An implant replaces the tooth root and doesn't affect neighboring teeth; a bridge relies on adjacent teeth for support.

  9. Can smokers get dental implants?

    Smoking can affect healing and implant success; Dr. Roy will discuss this during your consultation.

  10. What happens if an implant fails?

    Implant failure is uncommon but possible; if it occurs, the cause is evaluated and options such as re-treatment are discussed.

Root Canal Treatment (RCT)

  1. What is root canal treatment (RCT)?

    A procedure to remove infected or damaged pulp from inside a tooth, clean the canal, and seal it to save the natural tooth.

  2. Is root canal treatment painful?

    With modern anesthesia techniques, RCT is generally described as no more uncomfortable than a routine filling.

  3. How many sittings does RCT require?

    This varies by case complexity; some can be completed in a single sitting, others need multiple visits.

  4. Do I need a crown after root canal treatment?

    Most back teeth need a crown after RCT for long-term protection and function.

  5. Can a root canal treated tooth get infected again?

    It's uncommon but possible; regular checkups help catch any issues early.

  6. How do I know if I need a root canal?

    Signs include persistent tooth pain, sensitivity to hot/cold, swelling, or discoloration — a dental exam and X-ray confirm the diagnosis.

  7. Is extraction better than root canal treatment?

    Saving the natural tooth via RCT is generally preferred when possible, as it maintains natural chewing function and bite alignment.

  8. What is the cost difference between RCT and extraction?

    Please contact the clinic directly for current treatment costs, as they depend on the specific case.

  9. Can children undergo root canal treatment?

    Yes, a modified procedure (pulpotomy/pulpectomy) may be used for baby teeth depending on the case.

  10. How long does a root canal treated tooth last?

    With a proper crown and good oral hygiene, it can last many years to a lifetime.

OSMF & Oral Pathology

  1. What is OSMF (Oral Submucous Fibrosis)?

    A chronic condition, often linked to areca nut/tobacco use, causing stiffness and reduced mouth opening; early diagnosis and management are important.

  2. What are early signs of OSMF?

    Burning sensation while eating spicy food, reduced mouth opening, and stiffness in the cheek or mouth lining.

  3. Is OSMF reversible?

    Early-stage OSMF can often be managed effectively; advanced cases require more intensive treatment. Stopping the causative habit is essential.

  4. How is OSMF diagnosed?

    Through clinical examination, and sometimes biopsy, by a dentist experienced in oral pathology.

  5. Does Dr. Roy specialize in OSMF treatment?

    Yes, Dr. Sudip Roy has specific clinical experience managing OSMF cases at the clinic.

Aftercare & General Oral Health

  1. How often should I visit the dentist for checkups?

    Generally every 6 months, though your dentist may recommend a different frequency based on your oral health.

  2. What should I do after a tooth extraction?

    Follow the post-extraction instructions given by the clinic, including avoiding hard foods and maintaining gentle oral hygiene around the site.

  3. How do I care for my teeth after a smile makeover?

    Maintain regular brushing, flossing, avoid biting hard objects, and attend follow-up checkups as advised.

  4. Can poor oral hygiene affect overall health?

    Yes, oral health is linked to overall wellbeing; untreated dental infections can affect general health.

  5. What toothpaste and toothbrush are recommended?

    A soft-bristled brush and fluoride toothpaste are generally recommended; ask us for advice suited to your specific needs.

  6. How can I prevent cavities?

    Regular brushing, flossing, reduced sugar intake, and routine dental checkups help prevent cavities.

  7. What causes bad breath (halitosis)?

    Common causes include poor oral hygiene, gum disease, dry mouth, tobacco use, and certain foods; a dental exam can identify the specific cause.

  8. Can tobacco stains be removed from teeth?

    Yes, professional cleaning and stain removal treatments can significantly reduce tobacco and other surface stains.

  9. Is bleeding gums a serious problem?

    Bleeding gums can be an early sign of gum disease and should be evaluated rather than ignored.

  10. What is the treatment for sensitive teeth?

    Options range from desensitizing toothpaste to in-clinic treatments, depending on the underlying cause identified during examination.

Children & Family Dentistry

  1. At what age should a child first visit the dentist?

    Generally by their first birthday or when the first tooth appears, to establish good habits early.

  2. Do you treat children?

    Yes, the clinic provides preventive and family dentistry services for children.

  3. Are dental X-rays safe for children?

    Modern digital X-rays use very low radiation and are considered safe when clinically necessary, with appropriate protective measures.

  4. How can I help my child avoid cavities?

    Supervised brushing, limiting sugary snacks, and regular dental checkups from an early age.
