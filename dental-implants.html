What are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is the closest a patient can get to a natural, healthy tooth. It consists of three primary components: the Titanium Post (implanted into the jawbone), the Abutment (the connector), and the Prosthetic Restoration (the visible crown).

Because the titanium integrates directly with your bone through a biological process called osseointegration, the implant becomes a permanent part of your anatomy. This stops the bone resorption that naturally occurs after tooth loss, preserving your facial structure and preventing a "sunken" appearance.

The 5 Stages of the Implant Procedure

At our Pahari Para clinic, Dr. Sudip Roy follows a strict 5-stage protocol to ensure maximum safety and success:

  1. Initial Consultation & 3D Imaging: We perform a detailed oral exam and 3D CBCT scan to assess bone density and plan the exact placement angle.
  2. Surgical Placement: Under local anesthesia, the titanium post is precisely positioned in the jawbone. This is typically a 30-60 minute painless procedure.
  3. Osseointegration (Healing): Over 3 to 6 months, your jawbone grows around the implant, creating a foundation as strong as a natural root.
  4. Abutment Placement: A small connector is attached to the post to hold your new tooth in place.
  5. Final Restoration: A custom-made porcelain or zirconia crown is fixed, perfectly matching the color and shape of your natural teeth.
